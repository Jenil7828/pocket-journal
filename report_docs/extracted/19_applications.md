# 📄 Applications and Industry Relevance

## Real-world Use Cases

1. Personal self-monitoring and habit formation
   - Deliver concise summaries and trend indicators that help users review progress and maintain consistency in desired behaviours.

2. Digital mental health adjunct tools
   - Provide therapists and coaches with anonymized, consented summaries and mood trajectories to support session planning and outcome measurement.

3. Media personalization services
   - Integrate contextual recommendations into lifestyle or wellness applications that seek to align content with current emotional state.

4. Research in computational mental health
   - Supply structured extraction artifacts for longitudinal studies on mood dynamics, behavioural interventions and therapy outcomes.

5. Employee well-being platforms (with explicit consent)
   - Offer aggregated, privacy-preserving insights to organizational wellness programs while ensuring individual privacy controls.

## Industry Relevance

- Consumer wellness: Enhances journaling applications by enabling actionable recommendations and insights to increase user engagement and retention.
- Health-tech: Provides a pipeline for non-clinical, scalable monitoring that can feed into clinical research and augmentation with informed consent.
- Media and content providers: Offers contextual signals to refine recommendations and promote discovery in a mood-aware fashion.

## Deployment Considerations

- Regulatory: Systems used in health-adjacent contexts must follow local data protection and medical device regulations when outputs influence care.
- Business models: Privacy-first features support subscription or premium tiers for advanced insights and export capabilities.
- Integration: APIs and normalized schemas facilitate embedding the system into existing platforms as a microservice or SDK.

## User Personas

- Individual reflective users seeking better self-understanding and habit tracking.
- Mental health practitioners seeking structured patient narratives to complement clinical judgement.
- Product teams in media platforms aiming to personalize content beyond historical consumption.
- Researchers requiring structured, longitudinal datasets derived from naturalistic journal entries.
